# Brand register

When design IS the product: brand sites, landing pages, marketing surfaces, campaign pages, portfolios, long-form content.

## The brand slop test

If someone could look at this and say "AI made that" without hesitation, it's failed. Brand surfaces need a POV, a specific audience, a willingness to risk strangeness.

## Typography

### Font selection procedure

1. Write three concrete brand-voice words — "warm and mechanical", "calm and clinical", etc.
2. List three fonts you'd reach for by reflex. If any appear in the reflex-reject list, reject them.
3. Browse a real catalog with the three words in mind.
4. Cross-check — elegant doesn't mean serif, technical doesn't mean sans.

### Reflex-reject list

Fraunces · Newsreader · Playfair Display · Cormorant · Syne · Inter · DM Sans · Space Grotesk · Plus Jakarta Sans

### Reflex-reject aesthetic lanes

Currently saturated: **Editorial-typographic** (display serif + mono + ruled separators). By 2026, every Stripe-adjacent brand has landed here.

## Color

Brand surfaces can use Committed, Full palette, or Drenched strategies. Name a real reference before picking.

## Layout

- Asymmetric compositions for emphasis
- Fluid spacing with `clamp()` that breathes
- Left-aligned with asymmetric layouts feels more designed

## Brand bans

- Monospace as lazy shorthand for "technical"
- Large rounded-corner icons above every heading
- All-caps body copy
- Zero imagery on briefs that imply imagery

## Brand permissions

- Ambitious first-load motion
- Single-purpose viewports
- Typographic risk
- Unexpected color strategies
